Case Study 01 — Procore

A coded prototype that made an abstract vision real — and unlocked a platform.

Procore had talked about building a Construction Network for years but couldn't commit resources to something abstract. We made it tangible, secured full funding, and led the product from catalyst to launch.

Role Lead Product Designer
Company Procore
Type Zero-to-Launch
Scope Discovery → Design → Front-end
Status Live at network.procore.com

01 — Problem

The construction industry runs on relationships. The software didn't reflect that — at all.

Procore had a vision for a Construction Network — a platform connecting every layer of the workforce, from GCs to subcontractors to laborers — but it existed only in decks and conversations. Without something real to react to, stakeholders couldn't commit.

The fragmentation was total: GCs managed bids in spreadsheets, subcontractors chased relationships through phone calls, and Procore sat at the center of billions in project value with no way to connect the people doing the work.

"Procore had talked about building a Construction Network for years, but couldn't commit resources to an abstract vision."

03 - Discovery "The real world"

Customer Calls

We got on calls with customers and asked them to walk us through how their teams work today — real steps, tools, and handoffs, not hypotheticals. Those sessions surfaced where people actually look for partners, how opportunities show up, and what makes one firm more likely than another to get invited to bid.

We were trying to answer two fundamentals:
1. How do companies find other companies to work with?
2. How do companies position themselves to increase their chance of being chosen to bid on future projects?

Who was on the line: I led the calls, with our PM and our lead engineer on every session — design, product, and engineering hearing the same customer, together.

04 - Define

Define The High Level

Together with our PM and lead engineer, we worked at a very high level to map the key journey maps and flows in the Construction Network. The questions in front of us:
How will users flow through this experience?
How will they accomplish all that they need to accomplish?

Those high-level flows were put together with members of Product, UX, and Engineering in the room.

Case Study 02 — Scout Fuel

A production-quality prototype, built in a week and a half.

What happens when a designer closes Figma and ships code directly? You get a prototype engineers trust, stakeholders can react to, and a design loop that compresses months into days.

Role Solo Designer & Builder
Timeline 1.5 Weeks
Type Coded Prototype
Stack ShadCN · Tailwind · Cursor

4 Modern tools
0 Static mockups presented
100% Coded & interactive

Tech Stack

Cursor AI-native editor that collapsed the gap between design intent and working code. Kept iteration loops extremely tight.
ShadCN Production-quality UI primitives with full design control — no fighting a black-box component library.
Tailwind CSS Utility-first styling meant visual decisions happened directly in markup — design and code became the same document.
Tweak CN Rapid ShadCN theme customization. Locked in visual identity without custom CSS overhead.
